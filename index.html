Daily Philosophy

Japan does not have a cuisine. It has hundreds. Each destination in this guide covers the food culture, essential dishes, seasonal rhythms, drinking traditions, and dining etiquette of each location—the education you need before you sit down—followed by specific restaurant recommendations across every budget tier.

Explore Japan's Culinary Regions & Attractions

At a Glance

What Each City Does Best

Tokyo

The Capital of Endless Appetite

Edomae sushi, shoyu ramen, tempura, tonkatsu, yakitori, soba. The city of obsessive specialization.

7 regions

Osaka

The Nation's Kitchen

Takoyaki, okonomiyaki, kushikatsu, kitsune udon. Bold, saucy, affordable — the democratic table.

Kuidaore

Kyoto

The Refined Palate

Kaiseki, yudofu, yuba, obanzai, matcha. A thousand years of imperial and monastic culinary tradition.

Kaiseki

Kobe

Where East Met West

Kobe beef, akashiyaki, sobameshi, Nada sake. Port-city cosmopolitanism and legendary wagyu.

Wagyu

Fukuoka

Kyushu's Food Capital

Hakata tonkotsu ramen, motsu nabe, mentaiko, yatai stalls. Bold, generous, startlingly affordable.

Yatai

Oita

The Onsen Prefecture

Toriten, jigoku-mushi (hell steaming), kabosu citrus, seki mackerel. Japan's best-kept culinary secret.

Onsen

Kyushu

Regional Overview

Nagasaki champon, Kumamoto basashi, Kagoshima kurobuta, Miyazaki chicken nanban. A food world unto itself.

Island

Nara

Japan's First Permanent Capital

Sacred deer, Todai-ji's Great Buddha, Kasuga Taisha's 3000 lanterns. Ancient temples in a park where deer roam free.

Temples

Himeji

The White Heron Castle

Japan's best-preserved original castle. UNESCO World Heritage, 83 buildings, never destroyed by war or disaster.

Castle

Kurashiki

The White-Walled Merchant Quarter

Edo-era warehouses along canals, Ohara Museum of Art, Jeans Street. Japan’s most beautiful merchant district.

Heritage

Hiroshima

City of Peace

Peace Memorial Museum, Atomic Bomb Dome (UNESCO), Shukkeien Garden. A city rebuilt as a monument to peace.

Peace

Nagasaki

Where East Met West

Dejima trading post, Glover Garden, Peace Park, Inasa-yama night view. 400 years of international exchange.

Heritage

Beppu

The Seven Hells

Seven spectacular hot springs at 100°C — blood-red ponds, cobalt-blue pools, geysers, and crocodile farms.

Onsen

Takachiho

Where Gods Descended

Spectacular gorge with emerald waters, Mount Aso’s volcanic caldera. The mythological birthplace of Japan.

Mythology

Yakushima

The Ancient Cedar Forest

UNESCO primeval forest, Jomon Sugi (2200+ year-old cedar), Princess Mononoke’s moss forest. A living museum of nature.

Nature

Kagoshima

The Naples of the East

Active volcano Sakurajima, Shiroyama Park, site of the last samurai battle. Subtropical climate at Kyushu’s southern tip.

Volcano

Nikko

Lavish Mountain Shrines

Tosho-gu mausoleum (UNESCO), three wise monkeys, Kegon Falls. Ornate gold-and-lacquer opulence in a mountain forest.

Shrines
