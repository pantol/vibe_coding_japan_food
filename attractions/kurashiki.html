The White-Walled Merchant Quarter

Kurashiki is home to one of Japan’s most beautifully preserved Edo-era merchant districts. The Bikan Historical Quarter, with its white-walled warehouses lining a willow-draped canal, has been voted Japan’s most beautiful merchant quarter. Once a thriving rice trade hub in the 17th century, the district’s storehouses have been lovingly converted into museums, galleries, cafés, and boutiques. The city’s name itself tells its story: “kura” means warehouse and “shiki” means estate.

Kurashiki Bikan Historical Quarter

倉敷美観地区

A stunningly preserved Edo-era merchant district whose name comes from “kura” (warehouse) and “shiki” (estate). In the 17th century, this was a major rice trade hub, and the white warehouses (shirokabe) with their distinctive namako (sea cucumber) tile walls still line the canal today. The Ohara Museum of Art, founded in 1930, was Japan’s first private museum of Western art, housing masterworks by El Greco, Monet, and Renoir. Nearby, Ivy Square is an 1889 cotton factory transformed into a cultural complex of galleries, shops, and restaurants. Visitors can take a gondola ride along the canal for roughly ¥700. The nearby town of Kojima is recognized as the birthplace of Japanese denim, with its famous Jeans Street. The Bikan Quarter has been voted “most beautiful merchant quarter” in Japan.

Visitor Tip

Most shops and museums in the Bikan Quarter are open from 9:00 to 17:00, but the quarter is equally magical in the evening when the warehouses and canal are illuminated. Arrive early in the morning for the quietest experience and best photography light, then return after dark for the atmospheric evening illumination. If you’re interested in denim, take a short train ride to nearby Kojima to explore Jeans Street — the birthplace of Japanese denim craftsmanship.
