Kirishima

霧島

Sacred Volcanic Mountains

Overview Attractions
Attraction Guide

Where Heaven Meets Earth

Kirishima is a volcanic mountain range situated within Kirishima-Yaku National Park, straddling the border of Kagoshima and Miyazaki Prefectures in southern Kyushu. Its dramatic landscape of steaming craters, volcanic lakes, and ancient forests has drawn pilgrims and travelers for centuries, making it one of the most revered natural sites in southern Japan.

Kirishima holds a place of profound importance in Japanese mythology. According to the Kojiki (Ancient Chronicles) — the oldest surviving record of Japan’s history and myths — it was upon the peak of Takachiho-no-mine in Kirishima that the deity Ninigi-no-Mikoto descended from heaven to earth, carrying the sacred treasures that would establish the Japanese imperial line. This divine descent anchors Kirishima as one of the most spiritually significant landscapes in all of Japan.

The mountain range itself is a realm of primeval beauty: crater lakes shimmer in shades of emerald and cobalt, dense forests cloak the volcanic slopes, and hot springs bubble up from deep within the earth. For those seeking a place where nature and mythology are inseparable, Kirishima is without equal.

Onami Pond & Maruo Falls

大浪池・丸尾滝

Onami-no-ike is a volcanic crater lake nestled in the Kirishima mountains, surrounded by ancient forests. Its emerald water contrasts vividly with the lush surrounding vegetation, making it one of the most beautiful and accessible of the several volcanic lakes scattered across the Kirishima range. Nearby, Maruo Falls is a scenic waterfall tucked within the dense forest.

Natural Wonder ~2h walk
Onami Pond

Onami-no-ike is one of the highest-elevation crater lakes in Japan, sitting at approximately 1,239 meters above sea level. The lake fills an ancient volcanic crater and its still, emerald-green waters are framed by dense forests of deciduous and evergreen trees. The surrounding rim trail offers constantly changing perspectives of the water below, and on clear days the reflections of the sky and forest canopy create a mirror-like surface.

Maruo Falls

Maruo Falls is a scenic waterfall fed by hot spring water that cascades over a rocky ledge in the heart of Kirishima’s forest. The warm mineral water gives the falls a slightly misty, ethereal quality, especially in cooler months when steam rises from the cascade. The falls are easily accessible from the road and make a rewarding stop before or after hiking the crater lake trail.

Mythology

The Kirishima range is part of Kirishima-Yaku National Park and holds deep mythological significance. According to the Kojiki, the deity Ninigi-no-Mikoto descended from heaven upon the peak of Takachiho-no-mine in Kirishima, carrying the sacred treasures that would establish the Japanese imperial line. This divine descent makes the entire mountain range a place of profound spiritual importance.

Visitor Tip

Allow approximately two hours for a leisurely walk around Onami Pond and a visit to Maruo Falls. The crater rim trail is well-maintained but involves moderate elevation changes. The area is particularly stunning during autumn foliage season when the forests surrounding the lake turn brilliant shades of red and gold. Spring and autumn offer the most comfortable hiking conditions.

Visitor Tips — Kirishima

Kirishima is accessible by bus from Kagoshima or Kirishima-Jingu Station. The region is part of Kirishima-Yaku National Park, so trails are well-maintained. Check volcanic activity alerts before visiting, as some trails may be restricted during heightened volcanic periods. Spring and autumn offer the best weather and most vivid scenery. The area’s hot springs (onsen) make an excellent post-hike reward.
