The Nation's Kitchen

Osaka — Japan’s second largest city and undisputed food capital — has been known as “tenka no daidokoro” (the nation’s kitchen) since the Edo period, when it served as the country’s central rice-trade hub. The city’s powerful merchant class forged a democratic food culture built on bold flavors, generous portions, and communal eating that endures to this day.

Beyond its legendary street food scene of takoyaki, okonomiyaki, and kushikatsu, Osaka is a city of striking contrasts: ancient castles and futuristic skyscrapers, neon-drenched entertainment districts and serene riverside cherry blossom promenades. The surrounding region adds even more depth, from the ninja training grounds and cascading waterfalls of Akame to the forested trails and maple-leaf tempura of Minoo.

Whether you’re gazing out from a 300-meter observatory, exploring retro neighborhoods modeled on early 20th-century New York and Paris, or hiking through valleys home to giant salamanders, Osaka and its surroundings reward the curious traveler at every turn.

Osaka Castle

大阪城

Built in 1583 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi as the largest fortress of its time, Osaka Castle played a pivotal role in the unification of Japan. The current 1931 concrete reconstruction houses a museum with elevator access and a panoramic observation deck on the 8th floor.

Historic Landmark Osaka-jo
History

Originally built 1583 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi as the largest fortress of its era. Destroyed and rebuilt multiple times; current concrete version dates to 1931.

Highlights

Panoramic city view from the 8th floor observation deck. Nishinomaru Garden features approximately 600 cherry trees and is one of Osaka’s best hanami spots.

Tip

Buy tickets online on Klook to skip the queue. The museum inside covers the history of Toyotomi Hideyoshi and the castle’s dramatic past.

Dotonbori & Shinsaibashi-Suji

道頓堀

The heart of entertainment Osaka — a neon-lit canal district home to the iconic Glico Running Man sign (since 1935). The epicenter of street food: takoyaki, okonomiyaki, kushikatsu. Shinsaibashi-Suji stretches 600 meters as one of Japan’s longest covered shopping arcades.

Entertainment District Namba
Highlights

Iconic neon-lit canal with giant signs, the Glico Running Man billboard (since 1935), and an endless parade of street food stalls. Best experienced at dusk when the lights come alive.

Shopping

Shinsaibashi-Suji is one of the oldest and longest covered shopping arcades in Japan, stretching 600 meters with hundreds of shops and restaurants.

Food

Street food epicenter for takoyaki, okonomiyaki, and kushikatsu. This is Osaka’s “tenka no daidokoro” (the nation’s kitchen) at its most vibrant.

Umeda Sky Building

梅田スカイビル

A futuristic 173-meter skyscraper from 1993 designed by Hiroshi Hara. Two towers connected by a “floating” rooftop garden observatory offering a 360-degree panorama. Named one of the world’s top 20 buildings by the Times. At night, the floor glows with phosphorescent stars.

Architecture Panoramic Views
Architecture

Designed by Hiroshi Hara in 1993. Two towers connected by the “floating” Kuchu Teien (Garden in the Sky) rooftop observatory at 173 meters.

Highlights

360-degree panorama from the rooftop. Named one of the world’s 20 best buildings by the Times. In the evening, the observation floor glows with phosphorescent stars.

Location

Umeda district, northern Osaka. A striking landmark visible from across the city skyline.

Harukas 300

あべのハルカス

The tallest building in Osaka at 300 meters. The observatory spans floors 58–60, offering breathtaking views that on clear days extend to Awaji Island, Kobe, Nara, and Kyoto. The complex also houses the Abeno Harukas Art Museum.

Observation Deck 300m Skyscraper
Height

300 meters — the tallest building in Osaka. Observatory located on floors 58 through 60 with panoramic views in every direction.

Views

On clear days, visibility extends to Awaji Island, Kobe, Nara, and even Kyoto. Sunset and evening visits are particularly spectacular.

Museum

The Abeno Harukas Art Museum on the 16th floor hosts rotating exhibitions of Japanese and international art.

Shinsekai

新世界

A retro district built in 1912 as a futuristic city modeled on New York (north) and Paris (south). Home to Tsutenkaku Tower (103m) with the Billiken lucky god statue — touching his feet is said to bring luck. The undisputed capital of kushikatsu. Golden rule: NEVER double-dip!

Retro District Kushikatsu Mecca
History

Built 1912 as a futuristic city modeled on New York (northern half) and Paris (southern half). The retro atmosphere today is pure Osaka nostalgia.

Highlights

Tsutenkaku Tower (103m) with the Billiken lucky god statue — rub his feet for good luck. Neon signs, standing bars, and deep-fried everything.

Tip

Golden rule: NEVER double-dip your kushikatsu in the shared sauce! Use cabbage leaves to scoop extra sauce instead.

Namba Yasaka Jinja

難波八阪神社

A small Shinto shrine famous for its striking 12-meter giant lion head stage (ema-den). The lion is believed to “swallow” evil spirits and bring good fortune. A massive Instagram hit. Free admission, open 6:30–17:00.

Shrine Free Admission
Feature

The 12-meter lion head stage (ema-den) is the shrine’s iconic centerpiece. Its gaping mouth is said to swallow evil spirits and attract good luck.

Visiting

Free admission. Open daily 6:30–17:00. Located in the Namba district, easily accessible on foot from Dotonbori.

Tip

One of Osaka’s most photographed spots. Visit early morning for fewer crowds and the best light for photos.

Kema Sakuranomiya Park

毛馬桜之宮公園

A 4.2-kilometer riverside park stretching along the Okawa River, lined with over 4,700 cherry trees. One of the best hanami (cherry blossom viewing) spots in all of Osaka. Boat rentals available for viewing the blossoms from the water.

Cherry Blossoms Riverside Park
Scale

4.2 kilometers along the Okawa River with over 4,700 cherry trees creating a spectacular tunnel of blossoms in spring.

Activities

Boat rentals available for viewing the cherry blossoms from the river. Hanami picnics along the banks are a beloved spring tradition.

Best Time

Late March to early April for peak cherry blossom season. The park is beautiful year-round but truly magical in spring.

teamLab Botanical Garden

チームラボ

An interactive digital art installation at Nagai Park where plants and trees become living canvases for light projections at night. The displays change with the seasons, creating a different experience with each visit. Evening hours only; advance tickets required.

Digital Art Evening Only
Experience

Plants and trees are transformed into digital projections at night, creating an immersive fusion of nature and technology at Nagai Park.

Seasons

The installations change with the seasons, so each visit offers a unique experience. Spring cherry blossoms and autumn foliage are especially striking.

Tickets

Evening hours only. Advance tickets required — book online ahead of time as popular dates sell out quickly.

Denden Town

でんでんタウン

Osaka’s answer to Tokyo’s Akihabara. A district packed with electronics shops, anime and manga stores, retro game arcades, and figurine dealers. Otaku Road is the main strip. Maid cafes, retro consoles, and rare collectibles — all with fewer crowds than Tokyo.

Otaku Culture Electronics District
What to Find

Electronics, anime merchandise, manga, video games, figurines, retro consoles, trading cards, and cosplay supplies. Multiple floors of otaku paradise.

Otaku Road

The main strip concentrates the best shops for anime, manga, and gaming. Maid cafes and themed restaurants add to the experience.

Advantage

Less crowded and more relaxed than Tokyo’s Akihabara, with equally impressive selection and often better prices.

Akame 48 Waterfalls

赤目四十八滝

A valley in Mie Prefecture with a 4-kilometer trail along the Takigawa River passing a series of waterfalls. The five main falls are known as Akame Gobaku. Historically a ninja training ground for the Iga-ryu school. Home to Japanese giant salamanders. Museum included in the ¥1,000 entry fee.

Nature & History Day Trip · 3–4h
Waterfalls

Five main waterfalls (Akame Gobaku): Fudo, Senju, Nunobiki, Ninai, and Biwa. The name Akame (“red eyes”) comes from a legend of a monk meeting a fire deity on a red-eyed bull.

Wildlife

Japanese giant salamanders inhabit the rivers (up to 1.8m long, protected species). A museum at the entrance is included in the ¥1,000 admission ticket.

Tip

The trail takes 3–4 hours round trip. Good footwear is essential. Historically a ninja training ground for the Iga-ryu school (15th–18th century).

Minoo Park (Meiji no Mori)

箕面公園

A forested park on Osaka’s northern edge featuring the 33-meter Minoo Waterfall. A gentle 2.8-kilometer trail from the station winds past historic temples. The local specialty is momiji tempura — deep-fried maple leaves. Japanese macaques inhabit the forest. About 3 hours round trip.

Nature Day Trip · 3h
Trail

A gentle 2.8-kilometer walk from Minoo Station to the 33-meter waterfall. Pass historic temples along the way. About 3 hours round trip at a leisurely pace.

Highlights

33-meter Minoo Falls, spectacular autumn foliage, and resident Japanese macaques. Autumn is the most popular and photogenic season to visit.

Food

Try the local specialty: momiji tempura — deep-fried maple leaves! A unique and surprisingly delicious snack sold by vendors along the trail.
