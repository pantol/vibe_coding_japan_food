Japan’s Greatest Castle

Himeji Castle is the best-preserved original castle in Japan and a masterpiece of defensive architecture. Unlike most Japanese castles, which are concrete reconstructions, Himeji’s main keep and fortifications have survived intact since the early 17th century. Its brilliant white walls and elegant, soaring silhouette earned it the nickname Shirasagi-jō — the White Heron Castle.

Designated as one of Japan’s first UNESCO World Heritage Sites in 1993, Himeji Castle stands as a testament to the ingenuity of Japanese castle design. The complex includes 83 buildings connected by a labyrinthine network of gates, baileys, and winding paths — all designed to confuse and slow attackers. Its white lime plaster walls, fortified towers, and commanding hilltop position made it one of the most formidable fortresses in feudal Japan.

A visit to Himeji is essential for anyone traveling through the Kansai region. The castle is a 15-minute walk straight north from JR Himeji Station along a broad, tree-lined boulevard — visible from the moment you step off the train.

Himeji Castle (Himeji-jō)

姫路城

Called “White Heron” (Shirasagi-jō) for its brilliant white plastered walls and elegant silhouette, Himeji Castle is the best-preserved original castle in Japan and one of the country’s first UNESCO World Heritage Sites (1993).

UNESCO World Heritage Since 1609
History

Built in its current form in 1609 by Ikeda Terumasa. The castle has never been destroyed by war, earthquake, or fire — despite heavy bombing of Himeji city in 1945. One incendiary bomb fell inside the main keep but miraculously failed to explode.

Architecture

The complex includes 83 buildings connected by a labyrinthine system of gates and winding paths designed to disorient attackers. The white walls are covered with a special lime plaster called shikkui, which protects the wooden structure against fire. Atop the main tower stands a shachihoko statue — a mythical creature believed to protect against fire.

The View

From the top floor (6th story) of the main keep, visitors are rewarded with a panoramic view of the entire city of Himeji and the surrounding countryside.

Visiting

Hours: 9:00–16:00 (last entry). Tickets available on-site or online. The castle is a 15-minute walk straight north from JR Himeji Station. Arrive early to avoid crowds — the castle can reach capacity on busy days.
