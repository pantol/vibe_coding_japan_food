Overview Attractions
Southern Kyushu

Kagoshima

Kagoshima is dominated by the active volcano Sakurajima, located at the southern tip of Kyushu. The city sits across Kinko Bay from its iconic smoking peak — a constant, dramatic reminder of the volcanic forces that have shaped this region for millennia. Ash falls are a part of daily life here, and residents carry on with an almost casual familiarity with one of the most active volcanoes in Japan.

Kagoshima was the historical home of the powerful Satsuma clan, whose influence extended far beyond this southern domain. The Satsuma samurai played a decisive role in the overthrow of the Tokugawa shogunate and the Meiji Restoration that followed, fundamentally reshaping Japan into a modern nation. The city’s streets and monuments still bear witness to this era of revolution and transformation.

Often compared to Naples for its bay setting, volcanic backdrop, and warm subtropical climate, Kagoshima offers a distinctive blend of natural drama and deep historical significance found nowhere else in Japan.

Shiroyama Park & Sakurajima View

城山公園

Shiroyama hill (107m) in central Kagoshima with spectacular views of active volcano Sakurajima and Kinko Bay. Castle ruins and park surrounded by subtropical primeval forest. Historic site of samurai Saigo Takamori’s last battle in 1877.

Historic Viewpoint 107m
Satsuma Rebellion

Shiroyama is the historic site of Saigo Takamori’s last stand during the Satsuma Rebellion of 1877 — the final armed uprising of the samurai class against the new Meiji government. Saigo, considered the last true samurai, made his final charge down these slopes with his remaining followers. The event inspired the Hollywood film The Last Samurai starring Tom Cruise. A monument to Saigo Takamori stands near the base of the hill.

Sakurajima

Sakurajima is one of Japan’s most active volcanoes, smoking almost daily. Once an island in Kinko Bay, it became connected to the Osumi Peninsula after the massive eruption of 1914, when lava flows filled the narrow strait. The volcano remains under constant monitoring, and minor eruptions sending ash plumes into the sky are a regular occurrence.

The Park

The hilltop observation deck provides a sweeping panorama of Sakurajima, Kinko Bay, and the city below. The surrounding forest is designated a natural monument — a rare patch of subtropical primeval woodland in the heart of the city, home to over 600 species of tropical and temperate plants.

Practical Info

Hiking to the summit takes approximately 1.5 hours from the base. Best views are in the morning when Sakurajima is often cloud-free. The observation deck is also accessible by car or city bus. Free entry.

Tateyama Park — Cherry Blossom Festival

立山公園

Popular hanami spot in Kagoshima with views of Sakurajima. During cherry blossom season (late March – early April), the park hosts a festival with food stalls, drinks, and atmospheric evening illuminations among the blossoms.

Seasonal Festival Late March – Early April
Hanami Season

Tateyama Park is one of Kagoshima’s most beloved cherry blossom viewing spots. During peak bloom in late March to early April, hundreds of cherry trees burst into flower, creating a canopy of pink and white petals across the park’s hillside paths.

The Festival

The annual Cherry Blossom Festival transforms the park with food stalls offering local specialties, drinks, and seasonal treats. As evening falls, lanterns and illuminations light up the blossoms from below, creating an enchanting atmosphere for yozakura — nighttime cherry blossom viewing.

Views

The park’s elevated position provides views of Sakurajima across the bay, framed by flowering cherry trees — a quintessential Kagoshima scene that draws photographers and hanami groups from across the region.
