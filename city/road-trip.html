Kyushu Road Trip

Toll Cost Comparison

Driving across Kyushu is the best way to experience the island’s volcanic landscapes, hidden onsen towns, and regional food cultures that change every hundred kilometers. But Japan’s expressway tolls add up fast — a full loop can easily cost ¥20,000+ without the right pass.

This guide compares four options for a 7-day Kyushu road trip: paying individual tolls with an ETC card (via two different routes), the KEP (Kyushu Expressway Pass), and the TEP Kyushu (ToCoo! Expressway Pass). The differences are significant — the right choice saves over ¥6,000.

Option B: Coastal Route Scenic & Cheaper

Takes the Higashi-Kyushu Expressway along the east coast from Beppu to Kirishima. Several sections are toll-free (built under the New Direct Control System). Adds ~1–2 hours to the Beppu→Kirishima leg, but saves ~¥2,800.

Recommendation

Leave the car in Kagoshima, take the ferry as a foot passenger, and rent a separate car on Yakushima (~¥5,000–8,000/day). Taking your rental car on the ferry is very expensive.

The Verdict

TEP Kyushu 7-Day

At ¥15,330, the TEP Kyushu 7-day pass is the best value for this trip — saving over ¥6,000 compared to paying individual tolls with ETC, with broader coverage than KEP, and no worrying about tolls at all.
